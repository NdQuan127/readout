# Part I · Director's Statement

## 1.1 This is Not a "Feature Walkthrough"

The vast majority of SaaS update videos make the same mistake—treating the lens as a PowerPoint presentation. Open → swipe through 6 features → logo + slogan → end. Every second is spent "showing", and not a single second is spent "telling". When the audience leaves, what they remember isn't the product, but "yet another page that looks like it was generated by AI".

**This film must not do that.**

We want to tell a story. The story is only one line:

> **"md is the source code; everything else is the product."**

This is not a slogan; it is a worldview. Markdown is not "a lightweight markup format"—it is the source of writing. All downstream formats (html, docx, pdf, epub) are derivatives derived from this same source. huashu-md-html v2.0 extends this derivative chain from 4 to 6—but what is extended is not a "feature list", but the **radius of influence of the source**.

If the audience remembers only one thing after watching this film, I hope it is: "So it turns out md is the source code." How many features they remember from the list is just a bonus.

## 1.2 Contextual Dialogue of the Visual Language

Every good promo film engages in dialogue with a set of predecessors. I want this film to dialogue with the following context:

**Apple — "Designed by Apple in California" (2013)**

That film is the pinnacle of tech company promos in my mind. Director Mark Romanek got three things right:
1. **Pure white background + serif typography**—telling the audience that this is a film "about the design of design," not a demo
2. **Half-beat delay**—the subtitle for each sentence appears half a beat slower than the reader's reading speed, forcing the audience to linger
3. **Jony Ive's voiceover is almost like a whisper**—not selling, but sharing

Our film **has no voiceover**, so the first two principles must be amplified to 200% by typography and timing.

**Apple Silicon Launch Films (M1 / M2 / M3, 2020-2024)**

This series of short films taught me that **typography can dance**. The three characters of "M1" can fade out, appear, scale up, rotate, explode into dust, and then reassemble—the audience watches a logo become the protagonist of a choreography in 30 seconds.

**The hero of this film is not the product UI, but the two characters `md.` + an orange period**. It must become the protagonist of this choreography within 30 seconds.

**Anthropic Brand Identity (2024-2026)**

Anthropic made "terracotta orange + serif + geometric abstraction" an anti-slop benchmark for AI companies. It tells the industry: you can be a tech company, but you can also look like a small philosophical book published by Penguin Classics.

We inherit this color palette, but we need to be **more restrained**—Anthropic occasionally uses pure terracotta orange for large color blocks; our terracotta orange is always used strictly as an accent (occupying < 8% of the total screen area), leaving the remaining 92% to ivory white and ink black.

**Penguin Classics (from 1947, after Romek Marber's 1961 grid)**

Penguin taught me **the bravery of typography**. A book cover can be a large-font serif + a black horizontal line + no illustrations—and readers will stop for it.

The slogan reveal at seconds 25-29 borrows this language: **ONE SOURCE.** and **SIX FORMS.** are not "decorative text"; they are the visuals themselves.

**Pentagram (Paula Scher / Michael Bierut)**

Pentagram's signature is **information architecture**—the distance between letters, the distance from letters to borders, and the size ratios between text hierarchies are not determined "by intuition," but by mathematics.

Our grid system (Part II.3) comes from this tradition.

**Kenya Hara "White" (2008)**

Hara wrote: "White is not a color; it is a sensibility." (白は色彩ではなく、感受性なのだ)

The true protagonist of this film is not `md.`, but the **ivory white** surrounding it. Every shot must leave at least 60% of negative space. Negative space is not "not yet filled"; it is the content itself.

**Massimo Vignelli — Modernism in design**

Vignelli's 8-word maxim: "If you can design one thing, you can design everything." (能设计好一件东西，你就能设计好一切)

Our design system does not allow "adding a font on the fly in this scene" or "adding a corner radius value on the fly in that scene." All 12 shots share the exact same set of 5 color values, 3 fonts, and 4 easing curves.

## 1.3 Audience Profiles

Three categories of audience, ordered by importance:

**Primary Audience A · Existing users of huashu-md-html v1 (approx. 60% of traffic)**

They watch the film to find out "what's upgraded." Our promise to them: within 30 seconds, you must clearly know—
- New Capability 5: md → publication-grade PDF
- New Capability 6: md → standard EPUB
- The visual quality of these two capabilities is higher than expected (not just "I could do this with wkhtmltopdf" level)

→ Shot 08 and Shot 09 (3 seconds each) must feature a "★ NEW" label + the destination card must display **visible professional-grade details** like "printer crop marks" and "Apple Books frame"—making it immediately obvious to existing users that "this isn't a filler feature; it's a serious implementation."

**Secondary Audience B · AI-native creators who have heard of huashu-md-html but haven't used it (approx. 25%)**

They care about "what does this skill have to do with me." Our promise to them: within 30 seconds, you must realize—
- When you write articles, conduct research, or create white papers, **md should be your source of truth**
- 6 downstream formats, resolved with a single command

→ Shot 04 (any → md) must show them PDF/DOCX/PPTX/XLSX/HTML being absorbed into md together—this is the visual representation of "source-of-truth thinking."

**Tertiary Audience C · Designers, editors, and publishers who are completely unfamiliar (approx. 15%)**

What they see is a "beautiful tech short film" and they might not follow up. Our promise to them: within 30 seconds, you must be left with the impression that—
- What this creator/company makes **has publishing-house taste**
- It is different from the AI tools you've seen in the past

→ The anti-AI slop self-checklist (Part II.7) of the entire film is made exactly for them. Any purple gradients, emoji icons, or hand-drawn SVG characters will not appear under any circumstances.

## 1.4 Rhythm Philosophy

The pacing of Apple promos is not constant. It follows a curve of **half-beat delay — acceleration — peak — slow decay** (see Part III Emotional Curve for details).

Specifically for this film:

- **0-3s Half-beat / Slow pace**: The audience enters. The typography breathes character by character.
- **3-6s First Acceleration**: The `md` characters are born, and 6 file cards fly in one after another.
- **6-22s Second Acceleration**: 6 capabilities are shown in one breath, 3 seconds each without letting go.
- **22-26s Climax / Peak**: Slogan dual-line reveal, all chrome elements pulsating in sync.
- **26-30s Slow Decay**: The capability map slowly fades in, with the last second reserved for the brand seal + faint piano resonance.

**Key Decision**: The 22nd second is the climax of this film (not the 29th second). Second 29 is the resolution, while second 22 is the climax. Do not confuse the two.

## 1.5 What this film will NOT do (Anti-AI Slop Checklist)

Ordered by importance:

| Avoid | Rationale |
|------|------|
| No purple gradients | The universal formula for "technology feel" in training corpora; looks like cyber slop in 2026 |
| No emoji icons | The symptom of "using emojis to cover up lack of professionalism" |
| No SVG characters / hands / abstract figures | AI-generated SVG characters always have misplaced features and bizarre proportions |
| No Inter/Roboto/Arial for display | Too common, clashes with system fonts |
| No cyber neon / dark blue backgrounds (#0D1117) | Cliché copycats of the GitHub dark mode aesthetic |
| No stacking effects (blur/glow/particle) | An effect appearing twice is decorative; three times, it's slop |
| No Lorem ipsum | Every piece of placeholder text uses real, readable content (including hooks like "md is the source. Anything else is product.") |
| No stock photos | No real photographs appear in the entire film (it's about typography, not lifestyle) |
| No video progress bars, timecodes, or copyright attribution bars | These are player chrome, not content chrome—they will clash with external players |
| Do not make the `md` characters look identical in every scene | It needs 12 states across the 12 scenes, while maintaining the same core glyph |

>

# Part II · Visual System

## 2.1 Complete Color Palette

Not 3 colors, but 10. Every color has a **functional definition** (not just "use because it looks good").

```
Name            HEX        Role                               Max Area Ratio
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ivory paper    #FAFAF6    Primary background (ivory white, a touch of warmth) 60-70%
Mist           #F2EDE4    Secondary background layer (subtle darkness under card shadows) < 15%
Mica           #E6E1D6    Fine lines / dividers / card borders          < 5%
Smoke          #6B6B6B    Secondary text / metadata             < 5%
Cinder         #3D3530    Secondary dark (dark brown-black, not pure black)       < 10%
Ink            #1A1A1A    Primary black / main text                    20-25%
Charred        #2A2620    Extremely dark brown-black (for cover cards only)            < 5%
Terracotta     #C2410C    Main accent (Anthropic tone)         5-8%
Terra Hot      #E55D21    Highlight variant (flashes for a split second on the NEW label only) < 1%
Terra Deep     #8B2D08    Shadow variant (terracotta orange shadow/projection)         < 1%
```

**Ironclad Rules**:
- No color outside of the 10 listed above may appear in any scene. **No "adding a touch of cool gray on the fly in this scene."**
- The terracotta series (Terracotta + variants) combined must occupy < 10% of the screen area; otherwise, it will cause visual overload.
- Any text must strictly use one of the 4 colors: Ink / Cinder / Smoke / Terracotta.

## 2.3 Grid System

**Main Canvas**: 1920 × 1080

**Margins (Safe Zone)**: 80px top, bottom, left, right

**Main Content Area**: 1760 × 920

**12-column Grid**: column-width = 132px, gutter = 16px

**Baseline Grid**: 8px rhythm. All vertical positions must be multiples of 8 (unless there is a specific visual reason).

**Golden Ratio Anchor Points**:
- Upper 1/3 line: y = 360
- Lower 1/3 line: y = 720
- Centerline: y = 540 (default anchor for hero md)
- Golden ratio top: y = 412
- Golden ratio bottom: y = 668

**Critical Safety Zones**:
- Within top 60px: Chrome element area (capability counter, version chip)
- Within bottom 60px: Watermark / metadata area
- Central 800×600 area: Main content restricted zone (hero elements of each shot must fall within this area)

## 2.4 Animation System

**Easing Library** (4 in total, others are forbidden):

```
Name           Curve Formula                            Usage
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
expoOut       1 - 2^(-10t)                       Default ease (used for 90% of entrances)
overshoot     cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)  NEW badge pop-up / button reveal
linear        t                                   Background fade / paper texture translation
expoIn        2^(10(t-1))                        Exit ease (used for 10% of exits)
```

**Duration Dictionary**:

```
Event Type                Duration      Remarks
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Character stagger              30-50ms       Typing effect / slogan characters appearing sequentially
Small element entrance                300ms         file card / pill / chip
Medium element entrance                500ms         destination card / capability number
Hero element entrance             700-900ms     md character morph
Slogan character entrance           800ms         "ONE SOURCE." as a whole
Transition between scenes            300ms overlap cross-dissolve + scale
Exits                      200-300ms     Exiting is always faster than entering
```

**Stagger Rules**:
- When multiple elements enter simultaneously, adjacent elements have a delay of 30-80ms (not 0, and not exceeding 100ms)
- 6 pills entering: cumulative stagger of 250ms (50ms each)
- Slogan characters entering: cumulative stagger of 280ms (~30ms per character × 10 characters)

**Transitions between Scenes**:
- Always **cross-dissolve + soft scale** (no hard cuts)
- Previous scene in the last 300ms: opacity 1 → 0, scale 1 → 0.96
- Next scene in the first 300ms: opacity 0 → 1, scale 1.04 → 1
- 300ms overlap between scenes (on the timeline, the previous Sprite's end is 0.3s later than the next scene's start)

**Layers (each layer persists for 30s, intensity changes controlled by envelope)**:

- **L0 · Room tone** (00-30s): Extremely faint background noise, giving the screen a breathing sensation that is "not dead silent"
- **L1 · Piano single note** (00-08s): Single piano note repeating, once every 1.2 seconds, slowly building up
- **L2 · Piano arpeggio** (03-22s): Piano arpeggio enters, giving the feeling of "picking up the pace"
- **L3 · Cello drone** (08-22s): Low-frequency string pads, providing "weight"
- **L4 · Pulse** (15-22s): Extremely faint sub-kick, 4/4 rhythm (not a dance beat, but a cinematic pulse)
- **L5 · String swell** (22-26s): Full string section swells up to the climax
